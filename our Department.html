City Departments

City Departments & Roles

Department of Education & Youth

DepEd Ozamiz City is responsible for implementing national education policies and programs at the local level. In partnership with the local government and health organizations, DepEd Ozamiz has recently launched several school-based teen centers as part of the "Building Resilient and Aware Voices for Empowerment" (BRAVE) project. These centers provide confidential counseling, health information, and support on mental and reproductive health, demonstrating a strong focus on youth well-being and development.

Department of Health & Social Welfare

The Ozamiz City Health Office (CHO) and the City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) are the primary local government units responsible for health and social welfare services in Ozamiz City.

Department of Public Works & Infrastructure

Ozamiz City, public works and infrastructure responsibilities are shared between two main entities: the local City Engineer's Office (under the city government) and the national Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH), specifically its Misamis Occidental 2nd District Engineering Office.

Department of Tourism & Culture

The Ozamiz City Tourism Office and the Ozamiz City Local Culture and Arts Council are the primary bodies responsible for promoting tourism and preserving cultural heritage within the city.

Department of Business & Permits

The primary agency for business-related matters in Ozamiz City is the Integrated Business Permits and Licensing System (IBPLS) Office, also known as the Business Permit and Licensing Office (BPLO). This office is responsible for issuing Mayor's Permits and managing the city's business registration system.

Department of Environment & Waste Management

Ozamiz City, environmental management and waste operations are handled primarily by two entities: the local government's Solid Waste and Environmental Management Office (SWEMO) and the national government's Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR).
