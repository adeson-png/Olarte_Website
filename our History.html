Our History Of Ozamiz city

Historical Milestones

Pre-Colonial Era

Ozamiz was originally a small settlement along the Panguil Bay, inhabited by indigenous communities engaged in fishing, trade, and agriculture.

Spanish Colonial Period

In 1756, the Spanish established the Fuerte de la Concepcion y del Triunfo (Cotta Fort) to defend against Moro raids and to serve as a trading post.

American Era

During the early 1900s, Ozamiz (then known as Misamis) developed as an important trading and educational center under American rule.

City Charter

On June 16, 1948, Ozamiz was officially chartered as a city, providing self-governance and laying the foundation for modern urban development.

Modern Era

Today, Ozamiz City is a 2nd class component city in Misamis Occidental, known as the "Gateway to Northwestern Mindanao" and "The Gem of the Panguil Bay."
